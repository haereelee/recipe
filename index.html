KIMCHI FRIED RICE

    SERVES 2 or 1 if you want

    PREP TIME: 10 MIN

    COOKING TIME: 15-20 MIN

    TOTAL TIME: 25-30 MIN

    EATING TIME: Probably 5 minutes

COOKING STUFF

  • (home)skillet
  • a stove or some sort of fire
  • spatula or big spoon, or a stick
  • chopping board, probably
  • plates and bowls, optional, though recommended
  • utensils, also optional, though also recommended
  • napkins or paper towels, for the mess

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 1/2 cups of cooked short grain rice, cold one-day-old rice is best
  • 4 strips of bacon, or ham, or spam, or whatever leftover meat you have, diced
  • 1 cup of diced kimchi, best with a matured kimchi, not the teenager kimchi
  • 1/4 small onion, diced, just don't cry
  • 3-4 tablespoons of kimchi juice, not recommended as a juice
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, adjust to flavor or salt intake
  • 1-2 tablespoons of Korean chili pepper paste, gochujang, adjust to your spice level
  • Vegetable or canola oil for stir-frying
  • 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

OPTIONAL (but highly recommend)

  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • 2 eggs, fried
  • crumbled or slivered nori, seaweed

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Heat a lightly oiled large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped bacon or ham or spam pieces and cook until slightly brown but not crisp. Remove from pan. Remove excess oil if you don't want the high cholesterol.
  2. Add the kimchi, onion, kimchi juice, soy sauce, and gochujang. Stir-fry until the kimchi is soft and caramelized in about 5-6 minutes.
  3. Add the rice and meat in, and sesame oil, and mix everything together.
  4. Stir constantly so everything is well incorporated and the rice is broken up evenly. Add salt and pepper to taste. Scrape up the brown bits from the bottom of the pan while stirring for the wonderful flavors. Cook until the rice starts to brown and no longer sticks together.
  5. Serve with optional fried eggs and garnishes on top.
  6. Enjoy!

    For vegetarian options, just take out the meat and it's still delicious!

    Maybe add some more veggies, mushrooms, carrots, etc.

    Go with what feels right in your heart.
